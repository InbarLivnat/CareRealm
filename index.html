About CareRealm

CareRealm is a research initiative dedicated to understanding the role of care in sustaining social life. Across societies, care is often understood narrowly as a professional activity or a specific type of work. Yet care operates far beyond these boundaries.

CareRealm approaches care as a fundamental social process that connects institutions, professions, communities, and families. Care is not only something that happens in hospitals, welfare agencies, or social services. It is present in everyday relationships, in civil society organizations, in public institutions, and in the countless forms of invisible work that sustain collective life.

The initiative explores how care operates as a connective force across social domains. By examining the organization of care in welfare systems, professional environments, communities, and everyday life, CareRealm aims to illuminate the social fabric through which care sustains societies.

Theoretical Perspective

At the core of CareRealm lies a simple but powerful idea: care is not merely a form of labor, but a social condition that enables life to take place.

Care functions as a social fabric that binds together institutions, professions, communities, and households. Without care, everyday life would not be possible. Schools could not function, health systems would collapse, families could not sustain themselves, and social institutions would lose their foundations.

This perspective invites a shift in how care is studied. Rather than treating care solely as a professional activity, CareRealm examines care as a pervasive social force that shapes relationships, organizations, and social structures.

From this perspective, care connects domains that are often treated as separate: work and family, institutions and communities, professional responsibilities and everyday obligations. Understanding care therefore requires examining the intersections between welfare systems, labor, civil society, and social life.

Research Areas

CareRealm investigates the social organization of care across multiple domains of social life. The research program focuses on several interconnected areas:

  • Care Work and Welfare Professions – examining the experiences, practices, and institutional contexts of professionals working in care-related fields.
  • Gender and the Organization of Care – exploring how gender structures the distribution, recognition, and valuation of care work.
  • Civil Society and Nonprofit Organizations – studying how care is organized and practiced within voluntary organizations and community initiatives.
  • Social Work in Times of Crisis – investigating how care professionals navigate institutional challenges and ethical dilemmas during periods of social disruption.
  • Invisible Work – analyzing the hidden forms of labor and responsibility that sustain institutions and everyday life.
  • Boundaries Between Work and Life – exploring how care blurs the boundaries between professional roles, personal obligations, and civic engagement.

Research Projects

Current projects within CareRealm explore how care is produced and sustained across social institutions and everyday life.

Research projects examine the experiences of welfare professionals, the organizational structures that shape care work, and the role of civil society in sustaining social support systems.

Additional projects focus on the hidden infrastructures of care, including the informal practices and invisible labor that sustain institutions, organizations, and communities.
