About CareRealm

CareRealm is a research initiative dedicated to understanding care not only as a form of labor, but as a social force that sustains everyday life.

Across institutions, professions, families, and communities, care makes social life possible. It enables organizations to function, relationships to endure, and public systems to hold together. Yet despite its centrality, care often remains undervalued, unevenly distributed, and only partially visible.

CareRealm studies the social worlds of care across welfare systems, civil society, professional life, and everyday practices. It brings together research on care work, welfare professions, gender, invisible work, and the boundaries between home, work, and community.

The Idea

The core idea behind CareRealm is simple: care is the social glue that allows life to happen.

Care does not belong only to one profession, one institution, or one sphere of life. It circulates across formal and informal settings, across public systems and private arrangements, across paid work and moral obligation. This means that care should be understood as a foundational dimension of social life rather than a narrow occupational category.

From this perspective, the study of care opens broader questions about inequality, recognition, responsibility, institutions, gender, and the moral organization of society.

Research Areas

Care Work and Welfare Professions

Examining professional care roles, welfare institutions, and the lived realities of those who sustain systems of support.

Gender and the Organization of Care

Exploring how care is distributed, valued, and naturalized through gendered structures and expectations.

Civil Society and Nonprofit Organizations

Investigating how care is organized through associations, nonprofit actors, and community-based initiatives.

Invisible Work

Tracing the hidden forms of labor, coordination, and emotional responsibility that keep everyday life and institutions running.

Social Work in Times of Crisis

Studying how welfare professionals navigate uncertainty, overload, and moral demand in periods of disruption.

Boundaries Between Work, Home, and Community

Exploring how care blurs the lines between professional roles, personal life, and civic engagement.

Projects

Current and developing projects within CareRealm examine the social organization of care across multiple settings and scales.

  • Care work and welfare professions in institutional settings
  • Social work during crisis and prolonged disruption
  • Invisible work and care infrastructures
  • Mission-driven work across nonprofit and welfare sectors
  • Boundaries between professional care, volunteering, and everyday life
